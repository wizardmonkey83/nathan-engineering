Quadruped Robot Dog

Objectives

The primary objective of this project was to independently engineer a custom quadruped robot from the ground up, drawing inspiration from the open-source Dingo project. The goal was to master the end-to-end development pipeline for a complex robotic system, encompassing conceptualization, mechanical design, digital fabrication, and electrical systems integration. This initiative served as a practical application of engineering principles, moving from a CAD model to a tangible, remotely controlled prototype, and laying the groundwork for advanced software implementation.

Outcomes & Contributions

The principal outcome of this initiative is a fully functional, first-generation prototype of the quadruped robot, which successfully validates the custom-designed mechanical and electrical systems. As the sole designer and engineer, my individual contributions were comprehensive and spanned the entire project lifecycle:

  • Mechanical Design: I fully redesigned and modeled all mechanical components in CAD, creating a unique chassis and leg structure optimized for 3D printing, ease of assembly, and the specific hobby servos used.
  • Fabrication: I personally managed the fabrication of all structural parts using 3D printing, iterating on the design (as shown by the orange and blue prototypes) to improve tolerances, strength, and component fit.
  • Electrical Integration: I designed and executed the complete electrical wiring plan, integrating 8+ hobby servos with a central microcontroller. This involved creating wiring harnesses, managing power distribution, and ensuring reliable signal communication between the controller and all actuators.
  • Prototyping: The successful assembly and operation of the initial prototype represent the key deliverable, demonstrating a functional "Version 1.0" and providing a stable hardware platform for the next phase of software development and system optimization.
