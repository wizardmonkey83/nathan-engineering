Underwater ROV: BURST

Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to engineer a competition-ready, multidisciplinary underwater Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) from scratch as a core member of a UVA engineering club. The initial phase focused on mastering the fundamentals of underwater robotics, moving from a CAD concept to a functional, "Version 1.0" submersible. The project's ultimate goal is to strategically iterate and enhance this platformâ€”by adding advanced sensor suites and manipulation-capable actuatorsâ€”to successfully compete and excel in the 2026 MATE ROV Competition.

Outcomes & Contributions

The principal outcome is the successful design, fabrication, and in-water testing of the "BURST" ROV, which validates the core structural, propulsion, and electronic systems. As a leader within the club, my contributions have been both technical and managerial:

  • Design & Leadership (Structures and Safety): As the Lead of the Structures and Safety Sub-team, I was personally responsible for designing the ROV's primary frame and overall component layout in CAD. This involved optimizing for weight distribution, hydrodynamic efficiency, and service accessibility.
  • Fabrication & Assembly: I oversaw the complete construction and physical assembly of the ROV, translating the digital 3D models into a robust physical prototype using 3D design principles and mechatronic engineering practices.
  • Electrical Integration: I was an active contributor to the electronic assembly, assisting with the complex wiring of the propulsion system (motors) and integrating them with the central control and power systems housed within the waterproof enclosure.
  • Strategic Planning: In my new capacity as Lead Engineer, I am now responsible for defining the technical roadmap, setting engineering goals, and managing the team's efforts to develop the next-generation submersible for the 2026 MATE competition.

Technical Details & Skills

This project provides a deep, hands-on application of systems engineering, integrating multiple disciplines to function in a challenging underwater environment.

Mechanical & Mechatronic:

  • 3D Design (CAD): Proficiently used CAD software to model, analyze, and iterate on the complex, multi-part chassis and all structural components.
  • Robotic Assembly: Gained practical experience in the assembly of a complex mechatronic system, ensuring precise alignment of thrusters, ballast, and internal components.

Electronics & Systems:

  • Circuitry: Applied skills in electronics to contribute to the layout and assembly of the ROV's "brains," linking the microcontroller to the motor drivers and power supply.
  • Waterproofing: Gained critical experience in designing and sealing electronic enclosures to withstand water pressure and prevent catastrophic system failure.

Future Development & Goals:

  • Sensor Integration: The next development phase involves adding water quality sensors, which will require interfacing new hardware and writing software to read, log, and transmit environmental data.
  • Robotic Actuation: A key goal is the design and implementation of a robotic actuator (e.g., a gripper) to perform complex tasks like picking up and moving items, which demands a fusion of mechanical design, electronic control, and software programming.
